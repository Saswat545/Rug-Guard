Detection coverage
7 categories. 40+ signals.

Trained on confirmed rug pulls from 2020–2025. Every detection category maps to a real exploit pattern.

Fund-drain detection
Identifies functions that can withdraw the full contract balance, drain ERC-20 tokens, or call selfdestruct. Catches hidden drain paths in complex call chains.
CRITICAL
Ownership abuse
Detects hidden owner addresses, hash-based access control, proxy traps, and contracts that cannot renounce ownership — even after claiming to.
CRITICAL
Honeypot mechanics
Flags contracts where buying is enabled but selling is blocked — via explicit revert(), gas restrictions, or owner-only transfer permissions.
CRITICAL
Time-based rug patterns
Catches delayed drain mechanics using block.timestamp — contracts that appear safe at launch but unlock malicious functions after a set period.
HIGH
Proxy & upgradeability risk
Detects upgradeable proxies without timelocks — where the owner can silently swap the entire contract logic after launch, replacing safe code with malicious code.
HIGH
Code obfuscation
Flags assembly-level backdoors, keccak256 address hiding, encoded parameter obfuscation, and suspiciously low documentation ratios.
HIGH
Liquidity risk signals
Checks for LP lock status, unrestricted liquidity removal, burn mechanism verification, and vesting schedule presence — before you put money in.
HIGH
tx.origin & fallback traps
Classic phishing and execution traps — tx.origin in economic contexts, dangerous logic inside fallback() handlers, and gas-based execution restrictions.
CRITICAL
